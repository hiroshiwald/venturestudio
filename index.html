Entrepreneurial DNA
Urgency mapping. Where money moves, we build.
Most programs stop at product-market fit exercises. We go to the last mile: finding customers whose problems are urgent enough to pay now.
01

Motivation

Behavior over words. We look for customers who have already spent time, money, or effort trying to solve a problem. Actions reveal more than surveys.

02

Ability

Budget and organizational mapping. Who controls the money? Who influences the decision? Can the organization act? No budget, no deal.

03

Urgency

Driven by customers, CEOs, regulations, or competition. We find the external pressure that forces action. Without urgency, even great solutions wait.

How we work
Five stages. Revenue as the measure.
Start with the customer. Work backward to the science. Each gate requires evidence of demand.
01

Industry mapping

Deep interviews with business leaders. Map stakeholders, decision makers, and the urgencies driving their organizations.

02

Science match

Match validated urgencies with university research, algorithms, data capabilities, and faculty expertise.

03

Prototype

Build a proof of concept. Test with real users. Collect feedback. Iterate in rapid cycles measured in days.

04

Validate

Field deployment. Track usage and demand signals. The only validation that matters: customers pay and keep using it.

05

Spin out

Recruit a CEO and CTO. Structure licensing. Secure startup funding. Launch with proven traction.
